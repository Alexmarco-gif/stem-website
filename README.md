# Stem

## Getting Started

First, install the project dependencies:

```bash
npm install
```

Then run the development server:

```bash
npm run dev
```

Open [http://localhost:3000](http://localhost:3000) in your browser to see the site.

> **Note:** Always run `npm install` before `npm run dev` — especially after a fresh clone.  
> If you see `'next' is not recognized`, it means `node_modules` is missing and you need to run `npm install` first.

---

Stem is a decision intelligence platform built for fintech and financial-services teams in Nigeria. It helps teams detect meaningful market changes early and turn scattered signals into clear, actionable intelligence — so decisions get made with confidence, not guesswork.

## The Problem

Strategy and growth teams in fast-moving financial markets are constantly making high-stakes decisions, but the information they need is spread across dozens of sources. Competitor moves, policy changes, market shifts — by the time teams piece it all together, the window to act has often already passed.

The result is slow decisions, missed opportunities, and recommendations that are hard to defend.

## What Stem Does

Stem monitors the market signals that matter to your team, detects when something meaningful changes, and delivers prioritized intelligence you can actually use.

It covers four core areas:

- **Competitor Tracking** — Stay ahead of competitor launches, pricing shifts, and strategic moves before they catch you off guard
- **Market Monitoring** — Understand how your category is shifting and what it means for how you should move
- **Policy & Risk** — Surface regulatory and policy changes with enough context to know how to respond
- **Expansion Support** — Know whether the timing, conditions, and competitive landscape support your next growth move

The platform ingests signals, filters out the noise, synthesizes what matters, and delivers decision-ready outputs your team can review, share, and act on.

## Who It's For

Stem is built for strategy, growth, and competitive intelligence teams in Nigerian fintech and financial services — teams operating in markets that move fast, where signals are scattered and the cost of a blind spot is high.

## Why It Exists

Most intelligence tools weren't built with Nigerian financial markets in mind. Stem was shaped by conversations with 12+ fintech strategy leaders who needed something purpose-built for the unique pace and complexity of this market. The goal isn't more data — it's usable clarity that shortens the gap between detecting something important and deciding what to do about it.
